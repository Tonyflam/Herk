The thesis

A contest is a different game

Track 1 is scored on pure total return over one fixed week, with disqualification at a 30% drawdown gate. In poker's Independent Chip Model, chips aren't linear in prize equity — and equity in a trading tournament isn't either. The gate turns a blow-up into a zero, so the winning play is neither reckless max-risk nor the survival-first crouch most of the field defaults to. It's calibrated aggression with the gate as the only hard line.

⏱

Time left

Early in the week variance is cheap — build the stack. Late, with a lead, variance is the enemy — lock it in. Aggression tapers on a pre-committed clock.

🛡

Drawdown budget

The 30% gate is the only zero. As drawdown approaches it, HELM cuts risk convexly (budget1.3) — fast and early. Below it, it deploys: a return race rewards being invested.

♟

Rank posture

Behind near the end? Take bounded extra variance to reach the money — never enough to risk the gate. Ahead? Shed variance and defend the lead.

The edge

One auditable risk budget, every cycle

A vol-targeted cross-sectional momentum engine and a deterministic risk gate feed a Contest-Optimal Meta-Controller that folds the signal, the clock, the drawdown budget and rank into a single resolved gross cap and per-trade risk — written to a tamper-evident ledger anyone can re-verify offline.
